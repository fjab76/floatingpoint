Segment / ULP

Each normal double with a given unbiased exponent lies in a binade: a half-open interval [2e, 2e+1) (for positive numbers; sign is handled separately). Within that band, adjacent floats are evenly spaced. That spacing is one ULP (unit in the last place) for numbers in the segment. For the exact decimal value of any float, see Exact value.

ULP grows with magnitude: you have the same 53-bit precision, but the gap between neighbors scales with 2e.
